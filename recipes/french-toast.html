The best breads for French toast are brioche, sourdough, French bread, or challah. These varieties are dense and sturdy enough to handle total saturation in the wet, milky, egg custard without falling apart. However, French toast is traditionally made with day-old slices because they absorb the eggy mixture better than fresh ones (and this method prevents waste - it's a win-win). Of course, a slightly stale loaf is certainly not a requirement. If you only have fresh bread on hand, that'll work just fine.in a pinch, any thick-sliced white bread will do. Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
  • salt to taste
  • 6 thick bread slices
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, or more as needed

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  3. Lightly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat.
  4. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides.
  5. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  6. Serve hot.
